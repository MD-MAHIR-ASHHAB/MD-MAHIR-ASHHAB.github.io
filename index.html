About Me

I am a 2nd year Ph.D. student in Computer Science at the University of Virginia, focusing on low-latency network protocols (L4S) and real-time media systems (WebRTC). My research evaluates congestion control and end-to-end performance in multi-site testbeds.

Previously, I worked on memory management for LLM inference workloads, edge-cloud optimization, and NLP for social media content moderation. I am passionate about designing scalable, high-performance systems and applying them to real-world streaming and media applications.
