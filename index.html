About Me

I am a first-year PhD student in Computer Science at the University of Virginia. My research focuses on system-level challenges in large-scale machine learning workloads, with particular interest in memory management during LLM inference. I'm currently rotating with Prof. Haiying Shen.

My broader research interests include:

  • Memory Management for LLMs
  • Processing In Memory (PIM)
  • Serverless Computing
  • Networked Systems & Edge Computing

Research & Projects

  • Lookahead Caching for LLM Inference – Designing predictive caching policies to optimize structured workload performance.
  • Serverless LLMs Review – Explored system infrastructure and design for serverless inference.
  • NLP-based Moderation – Built classifiers to identify community guideline violations in social media text.
